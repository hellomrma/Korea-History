## Who was President Syngman Rhee?

Syngman Rhee was Korea's first president. After Japan withdrew, the Republic of Korea was founded in 1948 — and he became its first president.

Syngman Rhee built close ties with the United States and worked to safeguard the country. During the Korean War, the nation suffered greatly, but with American support it was preserved.

Later, however, he committed wrongdoing — rigging elections to hold on to power. Students and citizens rose up shouting "Step down!" — the **April 19 Revolution** — and he ultimately resigned and went to Hawaii.

## Life and Achievements

Syngman Rhee (1875–1965), born in Pyeongsan in Hwanghae Province, was the first, second, third, and fourth president of the Republic of Korea. In his youth he gained modern political knowledge through the Independence Club and study in the United States (PhD, Princeton University).

**Independence and the Provisional Government:** After the March 1st Movement he was elected the first president of the Provisional Government in Shanghai, but his petition to the League of Nations for trusteeship became contentious, and he was impeached. He continued diplomatic independence work in the United States.

**Founding of the ROK and the anti-communist line:** Rhee led the founding of a separate government in 1948 and became its first president. He set a strong anti-communist, pro-American course, and during the Korean War he opposed the armistice — friction with the United States.

**Long rule and the April Revolution:** He built foundations for prolonged rule through the *Balchwe* constitutional revision (direct election, 1952) and the *Sasaoip* revision (removing the limit on presidential reelection, 1954). The rigged election of March 15, 1960 ignited the April 19 Revolution; he resigned, went into exile in Hawaii, and died there in 1965.

## Historical Assessment

**Founding contribution and authoritarianism — a duality:** Rhee is among the most contested figures in modern Korean history. Supporters emphasize his role in preventing communization and founding a liberal-democratic Republic of Korea. Critics point out that under the banner of anti-communism he repressed not only the left but critical nationalists as well, and hollowed out institutional democracy. The Bodo League massacres (mass executions of those previously associated with the left at the start of the Korean War) are a representative case.

**The separate-founding debate:** The 1948 founding in the south alone was deeply controversial at the time. Kim Gu and Kim Kyu-sik, advocating a unified government, refused the South-only election. Critics argue that Rhee's choice solidified division; defenders see realism in the face of Soviet expansionism.

**New Right reassessment and ongoing debate:** Since the 2000s, scholars associated with the New Right have actively reassessed Rhee as a "father of the founding." The 2023 film *Birth of Korea* renewed public debate. Mainstream historiography responds that, while acknowledging his contributions, embellishing human-rights abuses and electoral fraud requires balanced narration. Assessment of Rhee remains an ongoing historical debate.
