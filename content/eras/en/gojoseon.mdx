## What was Gojoseon?

A very, very long time ago — more than 4,000 years ago — the first nation of the Korean people was founded. Its name was **Gojoseon**!

Gojoseon was founded by **Dangun Wanggeom**, said to be the grandson of Hwanin, the god of heaven. Dangun is said to have founded the kingdom under a sacred tree on Mount Taebaek and to have set his capital at **Asadal**.

The people of Gojoseon followed rules such as these:
- Do not injure another person
- Do not steal another person's belongings
- Do not wound others

These were known as the **Eight Article Code**. They show how advanced Gojoseon's civilization already was.

## The Founding and Development of Gojoseon

Gojoseon, founded in 2333 BCE by Dangun Wanggeom, was the first state in Korean history. A Bronze Age culture, it covered the northern Korean peninsula and parts of Manchuria.

**Founding myth:** According to tradition, Hwanung married a bear-woman, and their son Dangun Wanggeom set his capital at Asadal and founded "Joseon." The myth is not mere legend — it reflects the social structures of the time (sky worship, agriculture, tribal confederation).

**The Eight Article Code (Paljo Geumbeop):** Gojoseon's legal code regulated punishments for murder, injury, and theft. This shows that Gojoseon already had private property and a class society.

**Fall:** In 108 BCE, Han Wudi of the Han dynasty invaded and destroyed Gojoseon, replacing it with the Four Commanderies of Han.

## Reconstruction and Debates in Gojoseon History

Because of the severe scarcity of textual sources, Gojoseon remains one of the most contested periods in Korean historiography.

**Source criticism of the Dangun account:** The 2333 BCE founding date appears for the first time in Iryeon's *Samguk Yusa* (c. 1281), a Goryeo-era work. The earlier *Samguk Sagi* contains no such record, and "Joseon" appears in Chinese standard histories only from around the 7th century BCE. Some scholars therefore date the actual founding to the 10th–7th centuries BCE.

**Debate over territory:** Scholars in South Korea, North Korea, and China disagree on whether Gojoseon's center lay in Pyongyang (the traditional view) or in Liaodong (the Liaodong hypothesis).

**The character of Wiman Joseon:** Wiman Joseon, founded in 194 BCE when Wiman ousted King Jun, is debated as either a state established by Chinese migrants or one in which an indigenous polity adopted Chinese institutions.

> "Owing to the limits of the sources, a complete reconstruction of Gojoseon's history is impossible. For now, only history from roughly the 4th–3rd centuries BCE onward can be discussed with reasonable certainty." — Mainstream view in Korean ancient-history scholarship
